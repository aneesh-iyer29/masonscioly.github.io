ABOUT

Science Olympiad is a nation-wide competition that features 23 different science-related events. Our coach always compares it to a "track team for SCIENCE." Teams of 15 students compete in pairs or groups of 3 in the various events. They can range from building and engineering to study and lab, covering topics from physics and chemistry to biology and mechanical engineering.

Science Olympiad is a program that promotes strong team bonds, leadership, and above all else, fosters a passion for STEM.

our team

National Science Olympiad Tournament, June 1, 2019

At Mason, we compete in Division C at the high school. Our club was established in 2003 and since then, we've competed against national-level teams from Texas, Michigan, and even from here in Ohio.

We're one of the most popular, engaging, and rigorous competitive academic clubs at our school. Among 60 other teams, we have consistently ranked within the top 4 in the state for 5 years. In addition, we have dominated all other teams within the Southwest Cincinnati region, allowing us to have attended the State Tournament in our division at The Ohio State University for the past 9 years. More recently, we have been crowned State Champions at the Ohio State Tournament held on April 28th, 2018 and won the National-Runner-Up title the first time we attended Nationals at the University of Colorado on May 19, 2018. This past season, we qualified for Nationals at Cornell and succeeded in placing 4th overall with 7 placings total.
