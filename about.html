ABOUT

Science Olympiad is a nation-wide competition that features 23 different science-related events. Our coach always compares it to a "track team for SCIENCE." Teams of 15 students compete in pairs or groups of 3 in the various events. Each member is responsible for 2-5 events. They can range from building and engineering to study and lab covering the gamut of topics from physics and chemistry to biology and genetic engineering.

Science Olympiad is a program that promotes strong team bonds, leadership, and above all else, fosters a passion for STEM.

our team

University of Pennsylvania Invitational, Jan 2017

At Mason, we compete in Division C at the high school. Our club has been established for over 10 years and in those 10+ years, we've competed against national-level teams from Texas, Michigan, and even from here in Ohio.

We're one of the most popular, engaging, and rigorous competitive academic clubs at our school. Among the others, we have consistently ranked within the top 4 in the state for 4 years. In addition, we have dominated all other teams within the Southwest Cincinnati region, allowing us to have attended the State Tournament in our division at The Ohio State University for the past 8 years.

The Goal

However, we still have yet to reach our goal of qualifying for the National Tournament.

To qualify for Nationals, this year being held at the Colorado State University, we must finish within the top 2 at the State Tournament. We face tough competition at the State level as the other top 3 schools are National-ranked. But as mentioned before, we have consistently finished within the top 4, having been 2 points away from from 2nd this past season (16-17). We believe that this is the year we can finally make it.

We ask for your support in getting us there!
